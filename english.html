Welcome to HongYi Sinology

The "One Dragon" education concept proposed by Master Jingkong is a systematic educational model formed after his in-depth thinking on modern educational issues. It aims to cultivate talents with both moral integrity and practical ability. This concept emphasizes an integrated education system from prenatal education, early childhood education to lifelong learning, with Confucian, Buddhist, and Taoist culture as the core, focusing on moral cultivation and cause-and-effect education.

The goal of this concept is to return education to the essence of "teaching people to be human," achieving harmony of body and mind, society, and the world through lifelong continuous learning.

Our Education

Prenatal and Family Education
Emphasizes the significant impact of the mother's mindset, behavior, and environment during pregnancy on the fetus.
Encourages parents to set a good example and create a positive family atmosphere after the child is born, allowing the child to absorb positive values unconsciously.

Early Childhood Education (0-6 years)
Emphasizes the concept that "three years determine eighty," believing that a child's worldview, outlook on life, and values are established early on.
Uses classics such as "Standards for Being a Good Student and Child," "The Book of Responses and Retributions," and "Ten Good Deeds" as teaching materials to cultivate virtues such as filial piety, loyalty, and integrity.

Basic Education (Elementary to Middle School)
Balances traditional culture and modern knowledge, focusing on cultivating students' moral concepts and sense of responsibility.
Follows the principle of "reading a book a thousand times, its meaning will appear," advocating classic recitation to improve comprehension and memory.

Higher Education
Emphasizes the parallel development of academics and morality, avoiding the deviation of "talent without virtue."
Advocates integrating the wisdom of Confucianism, Buddhism, and Taoism to cultivate high-level talents with a sense of social responsibility.

Social Education (Lifelong Education)
Emphasizes that learning should not stop at school, promoting lifelong learning and continuous self-cultivation.
Centers on cause-and-effect education and moral education, helping the public understand the principle that "good deeds bring good rewards, evil deeds bring evil consequences."
